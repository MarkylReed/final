I met him when I was thirteen.
He scared the shit out of me.
I was used to being able to read people. I could read facial expressions and body language like a natural tongue, with all their little gestures.
I couldn't read him and it terrified me. I was scared of messing up. I was trying so hard to find what he wanted me to be so I could fit it-- and it never worked.
It changed when I caught him crying.
Asa isn't very expressive. He struggles with "performing" emotions. Seeing him cry was... different.
I rubbed his back and led him through breathing until he calmed down. He opened up to me about his anxiety, and a few other things.
I calmed down. Not because I suddenly knew what to do, but because I understood him. It took trial and error, but I eventually found a routine with him, and he let me take care of him. He trusted me with all of the little unsavory bits of himself.
He taught me that I didn't have to perform for everyone. It's a lesson I'm still learning, but he reminds me of it constantly. It's hard to miss with him.
How could I not love him?
